Art is my passion, compulsion, and career. The passion and compulsion were always there but the career took a several decades to come about. I have always created. Through childhood, adolescence, marriage, having children, college, work, more work, Dublin, Washington, California, Texas, Oregon, Missouri, Papua New Guinea, and back to Dublin, I still created art. My style and medium changed often. I see this long period not as time lost but as my path to explore my artistic voice by having no critics or expectations preventing me from experimenting.
I came to realize that I created as a means to understand how I experience and interact in the world through my emotions. Whether I was creating with paint, clay, wax, plastic, wood, steel, digital, or any number of found objects, even music, I was always expressing and trying to understand my emotions. I finally decided in 2017 to devote myself full-time to my artistic passion. The process that I use to create is to first map out the emotions from a significant event in my life. I explore each feeling and refine and group them and show their interactions. I end up with a diagram that represents the confusing and conflicting feelings about the event. (Interestingly, negative events are much more complex than happy events.) From this I create an individual sculpture or painting representing each individual emotion. Sometimes a viewer will immediately understand the emotion that I am representing, but often a completely different emotion will be experienced. These are the conversations that are very exciting to me. This is where I, as an artist, connect with a person on an emotional level. We share this world but we experience it differently. These differences are the beauty in each individual. I am exploring sculpture in resin and steel. The reclaimed and found steel that I use in my sculptures has an inherent power that helps to represent strong emotions. I work and manipulate the steel and combine it with epoxy resin to create interacting objects. These objects are then frozen together in resin creating a vignette of the emotion that it represents. The piece has its own shadows, colors, textures and unexpected interactions that enhance the experience. My new series of paintings use the ancient encaustic process. The encaustic hot wax technique was selected because the natural earthiness and depth in the beeswax helps to represent the mystery of the represented emotion. The process of pigmenting, layering, scraping, gouging, dripping and torching the wax creates the complex and essential interacting colors and textures. Please send me comments on the work you see. Your reaction to my work helps me understand the work, and myself more fully, but more importantly to get a little glimpse into who you are. Less
